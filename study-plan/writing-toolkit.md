## Task 2 (the essay, ~40 min, ≥250 words, aim 270–310)

### Step 1 — Identify the type, then pick the matching structure (most TR failures are using the wrong one)

| Prompt signal | Type | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| "To what extent do you agree?" / "Do you agree or disagree?" | **Opinion** | State ONE clear position → 2 body paras defending it (or partial-agreement with a concession) |
| "Discuss both views and give your own opinion" | **Discussion** | Para 1 = view A, Para 2 = view B; **your opinion in intro AND conclusion** |
| "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?" | **Adv/Disadv** | Para 1 = advantages, Para 2 = disadvantages; **give a verdict** in thesis + conclusion |
| "What are the causes…? / problems… / solutions…?" | **Problem–Solution** | Para 1 = causes/problems, Para 2 = solutions that **match** those causes |
| Two distinct questions | **Two-part** | One developed paragraph per question — **never merge** |

### Step 2 — The 4-paragraph skeleton

**Introduction (~40–50 words):** paraphrase the prompt (don't copy) + a thesis that previews your two reasons.
> *"It is sometimes argued that [paraphrase]. While [other side has merit], I firmly believe that [your position], primarily because of [reason 1] and [reason 2]."*

**Body paragraph × 2 — PEEL:**
- **P**oint — one clear topic sentence (the paragraph's single idea).
- **E**xplain — *why/how* it works (the mechanism). This is what most 6.0 essays skip.
- **E**xample — specific and plausible (invented examples are fine; real statistics not required).
- **L**ink — one sentence tying it back to the question.

> Develop **two ideas deeply**, not four shallowly. Depth = the difference between TR 6 and TR 7.

**Conclusion (~40 words):** restate your position + summarise the two reasons. **No new ideas.**
> *"In conclusion, although [concession], the benefits of [position] clearly outweigh the alternatives, chiefly because [reason 1] and [reason 2]."*

### Step 3 — Useful language (rotate; don't repeat the same connector)

- **Opinion:** From my perspective… · I am inclined to believe… · It is my firm conviction that… (stop reusing "In my opinion")
- **Add / contrast:** Furthermore · Moreover · However · Conversely · Nevertheless · That said
- **Cause / result:** As a consequence · This results in · Owing to this · Consequently
- **Concession:** Although… · While it is true that… · Admittedly…
- **Attribute views:** One school of thought suggests… · Proponents argue… · Critics contend…
- **Referencing (use instead of a linker on every sentence):** this trend · such measures · these factors · the former / the latter

Target **~5–10 well-placed cohesive devices total**. Over-linking ("Firstly… Secondly… Moreover… In addition…" every sentence) is a *Band 6 marker*.

### Band 6 → 7 checklist (tick all before you call an essay "done")

- [ ] Clear position stated in the intro and held **unchanged** through to the conclusion
- [ ] Each body paragraph = **one** central idea (no two arguments mixed)
- [ ] Every idea is **explained + exemplified**, not just asserted
- [ ] Linking is varied and natural, not mechanical; referencing used
- [ ] **Collocations are accurate** (don't reach for a rare word you can't place — "make a decision", not "do a decision")
- [ ] **Frequent error-free complex sentences** (relative clauses, conditionals, concession clauses)
- [ ] All parts of the prompt addressed; 270–310 words; formal tone; no contractions

### Common score-cappers (avoid)

Partial/off-topic answer (forgetting "give your own opinion", answering half a two-part question) · memorised templates (examiners disregard them) · mechanical over-linking · lists of undeveloped points · position that drifts · informal tone · under 250 words.

## Task 1 (the report, ~20 min, ≥150 words, aim 150–190)

Purely descriptive — **no opinion, no conclusion.** All chart types are possible (line, bar, pie, table, process, map, mixed) — practise each.

### Fixed 4-paragraph structure

1. **Introduction (1 sentence)** — paraphrase the prompt. Change *shows* → *illustrates / depicts / compares / gives information about*.
2. **Overview (the Band-7 gate)** — start with **"Overall,"** and state 2–3 *big-picture* features (highest/lowest, biggest change, start vs end, overall transformation, number of stages). **No specific numbers here.** An essay with no real overview typically falls to Band 4–5.
3. **Body 1** — a logically grouped set of data with **selected** figures + comparison.
4. **Body 2** — the remaining data with figures + comparison.

> Select and **group** the significant features — don't list every data point. Always **compare**, don't just describe.

### Language by chart type

**Trends (line/bar over time)** — use verb *and* noun forms:
- Up: rise / increase / grow / climb / surge — *a sharp rise*
- Down: fall / decline / drop / plummet — *a steady decline*
- Flat/var: level off / plateau / remain stable / fluctuate
- Degree: slightly · gradually · steadily · moderately (small) // significantly · considerably · substantially · markedly · sharply · dramatically (large)
- Compare: twice as high as · the highest figure · whereas / while · accounted for the largest proportion
- Approximate: approximately · just over · nearly · in the region of · stood at · reached a peak of

**Process diagram** — present simple **passive**, sequence markers:
> *"First, the raw material is collected. It is then crushed, before being heated to a high temperature. Having been cooled, the product is finally packaged."* Overview = number of stages + linear or cyclical + start/end.

**Map** — **past / past-perfect passive** + compass directions:
> *"was converted into · was replaced by · was demolished · was relocated to · underwent expansion"* · *"in the north-east · on the outskirts · adjacent to"*. Overview = the overall transformation (e.g. "rural → developed/urbanised").

### Tense cheat-sheet

| Visual | Tense |
|---|---|
| Past dates | Past simple |
| Future/forecast | "is projected/expected to" |
| Timeless process / proportions | Present simple (passive for process) |
| Map (before → after) | Past / past-perfect passive |

### T1 common mistakes

No/vague overview · numbers in the overview · data-dumping instead of grouping · describing without comparing · wrong/inconsistent tense · trend verbs on a map or process · over-linking · under 150 (penalised) or over ~200 words (steals Task 2 time).

## How to practise (the loop)

1. Write **timed** (40/20), on screen (you're computer-delivered).
2. Paste into **LexiBot** or **Speechful** → read the per-criterion feedback.
3. **Rewrite only the flagged sentences/paragraph** — this is where the band moves.
4. Compare to a **Cambridge 19/20/21 model answer** of the same type.
5. Add any new error to your personal "top 5 errors" list and hunt it next time.
