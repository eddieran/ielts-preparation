## The four things costing you the most (in priority order)

### 1. ❌ No overview — this alone caps Task Achievement at Band 5

This is the single biggest loss. There is no "Overall," sentence stating the 2–3 big-picture features. Your last sentence ("In summary, those tables are interesting information about people decisions...") is **not** an overview — it's vague, it gives no features, and it's an opinion.

**The overview is the gate to Band 7.** Put it as the *second paragraph*, right after the intro, and capture the big picture with **no specific numbers**:
> *Overall, the city's population grew enormously over the two centuries. At the same time, the distribution of residents shifted dramatically: whereas Manhattan held the great majority in 1800, by 2000 the other four districts together housed the vast majority.*

### 2. ❌ You included opinion and speculation — Task 1 must be 100% descriptive

Task 1 = report and compare the data. Nothing else. These all break that rule:
- "But what **interesting** is..." → your judgement
- "**it means that** people are moving out from Manhattan" → speculation about *cause* (the data doesn't say *why*)
- "**The question is where they go?**" → rhetorical question, wrong register
- "**Apparently**, they are moving out..." → inference
- "interesting information about **people decisions of where to live**" → interpretation

You may state *what* happened ("Manhattan's population fell between 1900 and 2000"). You may **not** explain *why* ("people moved out") or editorialise ("interesting"). Cut all of it.

### 3. ❌ No paragraphs — it's one block of text

Coherence & Cohesion needs four clear paragraphs: **Intro → Overview → Body 1 → Body 2.** A single block can't score above ~6 on CC no matter how good the sentences are.

### 4. ❌ Tense — past data needs the past tense

1800 and 1900 are finished, so use **past simple**. You used the present throughout:
- "The population of whole NYC **is** only 79216 in 1800" → **was**
- "76% of population **lived** ... the number **is** 60 thousands" → **was**
- "people **are moving out** ... since 1900" → **had been moving / moved** (and cut, per #2)

Use present simple only for the timeless percentages if you frame them generally; for dated figures, past.

## Band 7.5+ model answer (same data, ~170 words)

```
The three tables compare the total population of New York City with the
populations of Manhattan and of the city's four other districts (Brooklyn,
the Bronx, Queens and Staten Island) in 1800, 1900 and 2000, expressed both
as figures and as percentages of the total.

Overall, the city's population grew enormously over the two centuries. At the
same time, the distribution of residents shifted dramatically: whereas
Manhattan was home to the great majority of the population in 1800, by 2000
the four other districts together housed the vast majority.

In 1800, Manhattan was dominant, with 60,515 residents, or 76% of the total,
compared with only 18,701 people (24%) in the other districts. Its population
then surged to 1,850,093 by 1900, although its share had already dropped to 54%.

By 2000, however, the number of people living in Manhattan had fallen slightly
to 1,538,096, accounting for just 19% of the total. The other districts, by
contrast, grew continuously throughout the period, reaching 6,471,089, or 81%
of New York's roughly eight million inhabitants.
```

**Why this scores ~7.5:**
- **Intro** paraphrases the prompt (compare/illustrate, "expressed as figures and percentages") instead of copying.
- **Overview** = paragraph 2, "Overall," + two big features, **no numbers**.
- **Paragraphing**: intro / overview / Manhattan / other districts.
- **Tense**: past throughout (was, surged, had dropped, had fallen) because all dates are finished; note **past perfect** ("had already dropped") to sequence events.
- **Comparison language**: "compared with", "whereas", "by contrast", "or 76% of the total".
- **Proportion verbs**: "accounting for", "representing", "or 81%".
- **No opinion, no conclusion** — pure description.
- **Referencing**: "Its population", "the other districts", "that had reversed".
