## The 5 things costing you the most (priority order)

### 1. ❌ Your essay argues against itself — this alone caps Task Response at 5

All three examiners led with this. You wrote:

> "In my opinion, this assertion is largely **unfounded**" *(= I disagree, water should NOT be free)*
> …then Body 1: "The most compelling reason for **agreeing** is that…"

You took the frame `The most compelling reason for [agreeing / disagreeing]` and **chose the wrong side of the slash.** To an examiner, your essay now says "I disagree… here's why I agree" — the position reads as self-contradictory, and a contradicted position cannot score above 5 no matter how good the English is.

**Fix:** `The strongest objection to free universal water is that…` Whenever a frame has a `/`, **delete the option you don't mean** before you write on.

### 2. ❌ "Such cases" and "This example confirms" — but you gave no example

> "**Such cases** make it clear that…" · "**This example** confirms that…"

These are *link-back* frames whose whole job is to point at evidence. You deployed both, but **there is not a single concrete example anywhere in the essay.** The frame promises proof and delivers an empty pointer — examiners notice instantly, and "no concrete examples" is the other reason TR is stuck at 5.

**Fix:** every body paragraph needs one real, specific example. The rewrite below adds *Singapore's NEWater plant* and *South Africa's free basic water allowance* — invented-but-plausible is fine; you just have to actually supply it.

### 3. ❌ Body 2 is a concession dressed up as a "second reason"

You labelled it "A second, equally important consideration," but its content — "the government should provide free/subsidised water to the poor" — actually **concedes part of the opposing view.** Two body paragraphs that pull in different directions (one says "don't make it free," the other says "make it free for some") read as a wobbling position.

**Fix:** signpost it honestly as a *qualification that still supports disagree*: "This does not mean the poor should be abandoned — but protecting them is a **narrow exception within a paid system**, not a reason to make water free for everyone."

### 4. ❌ Frequent spelling + grammar errors cap LR and GRA at 5

Seven misspellings in 300 words (**acess, assertation, effeient, transpotation, Undoubtly, affort, Majorly**), plus comma splices ("marked with price, price is…"), agreement faults ("people who really needs"), and a final sentence that breaks down completely ("Majorly reasons I support is price should be decided…"). Individually small; collectively they define Band 5 for two of the four criteria.

### 5. ❌ Your intro copies the prompt almost word-for-word

> Prompt: "Access to clean water is a basic human right. Therefore every home should have a water supply that is provided free of charge."
> You: "acess to clean water is a basic human right, therefore every home should have a water supply that is provided free of charge."

Copied words **don't count toward your 300** and signal weak Lexical Resource. Paraphrase it: *"Few would dispute that clean water is essential to life, and some argue it should reach every home free of charge."*

## The meta-lesson: you used the templates — now fill them correctly

You clearly drafted from [`task2-sentence-frames.md`](../../task2-sentence-frames.md). The frames did their job (4 clean paragraphs, clear thesis structure). The marks leaked at the **fill-in** stage. Run this 4-point check on every future essay (it's the checklist at the bottom of that file):

1. **Choose the slash.** `[agreeing / disagreeing]`, `[positive / negative]` — delete the one you don't mean. (This cost you the most today.)
2. **Deliver the evidence the frame points to.** If you write "Such cases" / "This example," there must be an actual case/example above it.
3. **Reword the overused openers.** "It is frequently argued that…" is the one phrase the file explicitly says to reword — don't paste it raw.
4. **Don't copy the prompt.** Paraphrase the question; copied words don't count.

## Band 7.5 model answer — *your arguments, fixed*

This keeps **your** two ideas (cost/market-pricing + protecting the poor). Nothing here is a "better" argument — it's *your* argument, executed at target level. (~290 words; independently verified at Band 7.5.)

```
Few would dispute that clean water is essential to human life, and some go
further, insisting that it should be piped into every household entirely free
of charge. I find this proposal largely unconvincing. Supplying safe water
involves genuine costs, which makes pricing a sensible mechanism; the real
obligation of the state is not to abolish charges altogether but to shield
those who cannot afford to pay.

The strongest objection to free universal water is that modern economies depend
on prices to balance supply and demand, and water is no exception. Delivering a
drinkable glass of water requires extraction, filtration, treatment and
transport, all of which consume labour and resources, while genuinely clean
sources in nature are scarce. Singapore's NEWater programme illustrates this:
reclaiming and purifying used water demands costly plants and skilled engineers.
If water were free for everyone, who would fund the workers and infrastructure
that make it safe? A price, therefore, is the most efficient way to sustain the
system.

This does not mean the poor should be abandoned. Water should not be universally
free, but the government must ensure that vulnerable households are never priced
out — though such protections are narrow exceptions within a paid system, not a
move towards free supply for all. The state can regulate tariffs so that they do
not rise unreasonably; South Africa, for instance, guarantees a free basic
allowance of water per household and charges only for additional use. In
addition, targeted subsidies funded through taxation can cover those who still
cannot pay.

In conclusion, while clean water is a basic right and the poorest deserve
protection, I remain convinced that a wholly free supply is neither sustainable
nor efficient. Prices should be set by the market and carefully regulated by the
government.
```

**Why this scores ~7.5 (and what changed):**
- **Stance fixed:** "the most compelling reason for *agreeing*" → "the strongest **objection**." DISAGREE is now consistent across intro, both bodies, and conclusion. *(TR 5 → 7)*
- **Real examples added:** Singapore NEWater + South Africa's free basic water — the "Such cases"/"This example" frames now actually have cases. *(TR + CC)*
- **Body 2 reframed** as an explicit concession ("narrow exceptions within a paid system"), so both paragraphs now pull toward *disagree*. *(CC)*
- **Two complementary measures, not a fake contrast:** "On the one hand/On the other hand" → "The state can regulate… **In addition,** subsidies…" *(CC)*
- **Register + collocation:** "a lot of procedures", "go crazy to hurt poor people" → "extraction, filtration, treatment and transport", "do not rise unreasonably." *(LR)*
- **Spelling/grammar clean:** every misspelling corrected, comma splices removed, contractions gone, and the collapsed final sentence rebuilt. *(LR + GRA)*

## Your 5-point action list

1. **Kill the slash-option error.** Before writing a body paragraph, reread your thesis and confirm the topic sentence points the *same* way. (Today: "agreeing" inside a disagree essay = automatic Band 5.)
2. **One concrete example per body paragraph — always.** A named country, scheme, or scenario. No "Such cases" without a case.
3. **Spelling drill.** Make a personal list of your 7 misses (access, assertion, efficient, transport, undoubtedly, afford, major) and write each 5×. These are free marks.
4. **Eliminate comma splices.** Two complete sentences need a full stop or semicolon, never a comma. ("marked with price, price is…" ✗)
5. **Paraphrase the prompt — never copy it.** Train one reflex opener: "Few would dispute that…" / "It is sometimes claimed that…" then reword the rest.

**Drill:** rewrite *this same prompt* from scratch, timed 40 minutes, applying all five fixes — then compare to the model above. Re-doing the same question with the fixes locks them in faster than a new prompt. Then run it through 2 AI checkers and confirm the band has moved into the 6s.
